CU iClicker for students attending the live lectures

What is an iClicker?

iClicker is an in-class student response system designed to better engage students in the learning process and also provide instant feedback to the student. Past research has shown the clicker to be very effective at improving student/lecturer interactions, which in turn are known to improve student learning (which is what we care about) and, consequently, student grades (what you care about).

How does it work?

Each student uses a "clicker" web account (called iClicker Reef) that allows her or him to vote on professor's questions by clicking on the appropriate answer choice. iClicker Reef is software students can use to review clicker questions and answers, as well as vote in a remote learning environment. Students can use iClicker Reef on their mobile device or computer.

The results are gathered instantly and the professor can display results in a graph in class, or refer them later for analysis, grading, etc.

Why do we use it?

We want you to learn more and enjoy the class and research suggests clickers can help:

  • Students come to class more often when clickers are used (Cue, 1998; Jackson & Trees, 2003; Cadwell, 2007)
  • A majority of students enjoy using clickers (Simpson & Oliver, 2006; Stowell & Nelson, 2007)
  • Students are more alert and interested in class (Burnstein & Lederman, 2001; Preszler, Dawe, Schuster, & Schuster, 2007)
  • Student get higher final grades when clickers are used (Freeman et al., 2007; Hall et al., 2005; Nguyen et al., 2006)
  • Students do better on tests (Caraghan & Webb, 2005; Crossgrove & Curran, 2008; Suchman et al., 2006)
  • Students retain more information from lecture (Cambell & Meyer, 2008; Luke et al., 2010; Mun, Hew & Cheung, 2008; Pradhan et al. 2005)
  • Information is retained longer (Luke et al., 2010)

How do I get points?

Credit is given for responses (right or wrong), with points for each lecture. The number of lectures in which the clicker is used is N, but you will be graded on only N-3: the lowest three grades will be dropped. Your clicker grade is then the sum of your points.

Each lecture is worth 2 points. Students get 1 point for "participation" and 0.25 performance points for each correct answer with a maximum of 1 performance point / lecture.
